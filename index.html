John Entwistle

“Thunderfingers”

Here's a timeline of John Entwistle's life:

  • 1944 - John Alec Entwistle is born in Chiswick, London, England.
  • 1959 - John forms The Confederates with Pete Townshend at Acton County School. John plays the French horn and Pete plays the banjo.
  • 1961 - John joins The Detours after being recruited by Roger Daltrey. John plays bass guitar.
  • 1962 - John suggests Pete Townshend join the Detours, and Pete joins the band.
  • 1964 - The Detours change their name to The Who, with Keith Moon joining on drums. The band is briefly marketed as The High Numbers before reverting to The Who.
  • 1965 - The Who release their first single, "I Can't Explain." Later that year, they release "My Generation", with John playing one of the first bass guitar solos in rock history.
  • 1966 - The Who release the album "A Quick One", with John contributing "Boris the Spider" and "Whiskey Man". The chorus of "Boris the Spider" (sung in basso profundo) is thought to be the precursor to the "death growl" technique used in black metal. "Boris the Spider" becomes a staple at live shows.
  • 1967 - John marries Alison Wise. The Who release the album "The Who Sell Out", with John writing "Silas Stingy", "Medac", "Heinz Baked Beans", and several jingles.
  • 1969 - The Who release the album "Tommy", with John contributing the songs "Cousin Kevin" and "Fiddle About".
  • 1970 - The Who release the live album "Live at Leeds", regarded as one of the greatest live rock albums of all time. The album features John's thundering bass guitar skills and gift for musical improvisation.
  • 1971 - John releases his first solo album, "Smash Your Head Against The Wall". The Who release the album "Who's Next", with the song "My Wife" written by John. Later that year, John's son Christopher is born.
  • 1972 - John releases his second solo album, "Whistle Rymes".
  • 1973 - The Who release "Quadrophenia", with John providing brass arrangements for the album and delivering a strong bass guitar performance on "The Real Me". John releases the solo album "Rigor Mortis Sets in".
  • 1975 - The Who release "The Who by Numbers", with John contributing the song "Success Story" and the album artwork. John releases "Mad Dog" with the band "John Entwistle's Ox".
  • 1978 - The Who release "Who Are You", with John writing "905", "Had Enough", and "Trick of the Light".
  • 1981 - The Who release "Face Dances", with John composing "The Quiet One" and "You". John releases the solo album "Too Late the Hero", with Joe Walsh of The Eagles on guitar.
  • 1982 - The Who release "It's Hard", with John contributing "It's Your Turn", "Dangerous", and "One at a Time". John's bass guitar work on "Eminence Front" is regarded as one of the greatest rock bass performances of all time.
  • 1996 - John releases the solo album "The Rock".
  • 2000 - John is named "Bassist of the Millennium" in Guitar magazine's reader's poll. John releases "Music from Van-Pires" with The John Entwistle Band.
  • 2002 - John dies at the age of 57 in Paradise, Nevada.
